Overview: A paired-programming project to create a text style, turn-based adventure game. By linking multiple Java methods, the final product was a player-choice driven game supplemented with ASCII art for visual impact. Credit to: https://github.com/JoshMarchand for his contributions.

Upon program execution, user will be greeted by an image of a castle with a Start prompt (Do you want to enter?)

After confirming, they will be asked to enter their name and an enemy will appear, initiating the turn-based fighting game.

The player will then have the option of selecting their weapon, each of which has its own unique buff property:

Sword = increases critical hit chance.

Axe = increases attack power.

Mace = increases defense level.

After selecting their weapon, the player is then prompted with what action to take: Attack will allow for one hit opportunity from both the player and enemy; Defend will halve incoming enemy attack damage...

...and Potion will replenish player hit points (HP).

In the background of each fight, a "Critical Hit" percentage is always present, which may present itself on attack.

Also running in the background, if the battle meets or exceeds 6 "turns" by the player, the special move Kiss of Death! appears as an option. This spell will reduce enemy's HP to 1, allowing for a swift end to the fight.

After vanquishing the enemy, the player is shown a victory message and symbol.

If defeated, they will see a different message and image.
